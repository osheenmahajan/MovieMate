Oppenheimer

Rating and Description

"Oppenheimer" is a biographical drama that chronicles the life of J. Robert Oppenheimer, the brilliant physicist behind the development of the atomic bomb during World War II. Directed by Christopher Nolan, the film delves into the complexities of Oppenheimer's character, his moral struggles, and the immense pressure of leading the Manhattan Project. With a strong performance by the lead actor and a gripping narrative, "Oppenheimer" offers a thought-provoking exploration of the ethical dilemmas surrounding scientific advancement and the devastating impact of nuclear weapons. The film is a powerful reflection on the responsibilities that come with knowledge and the consequences of innovation.
